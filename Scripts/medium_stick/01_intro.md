### What is Medium Stick

    Hey everyone! Today, we’re talking about one of the coolest, most practical weapons in Kung Fu: the Medium Stick.

    Now, a lot of people think martial arts staffs are always super long, like the ones that reach your eyebrows. But this one? It’s the Goldilocks weapon. Not too long, not too short—it’s just right.

### What does a medium stick look like?

    ## Length 
    Rule number one: Height. When it’s standing right next to me, the top should line up perfectly with my chest. Boom. Right there. This makes it super fast for close-quarters blocking.

    Rule number two: The '13 grips.' If you stack your hands grip-by-grip all the way up... [Count quickly] ...1, 2, 3... it should take exactly thirteen hand-widths to get from one end to the other. Perfect proportions for your upper body.

    And rule number three: The arm span. If I stretch one arm all the way out, and bend my other elbow across my chest, the stick should span exactly from this elbow to those fingertips.

### Shape
    you need to know your stick anatomy. 
    Traditional Kung Fu sticks aren't perfectly even cylinders.
    they actually taper.

    You have the Big End [point to the thicker base] and the Little End [point to the thinner tip]. Think of the Big End as your anchor—it stays closer to your body for stability and weight. The Little End is your whip—it flashes out for lightning-fast strikes.


### how do you actually hold it
    This is where Kung Fu philosophy comes in with Yin and Yang grips.

    If your palm is facing up toward the sky, that’s a Yang grip [flip one hand palm-up]. It’s open, active, and drives power. If your palm is facing down toward the earth, that’s a Yin grip [flip the other hand palm-down]. It’s protective, hidden, and great for quick blocks."

    When you fight, your hands constantly switch between Yin and Yang, pushing and pulling to generate massive leverage without needing a ton of space.
